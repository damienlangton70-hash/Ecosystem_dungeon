# The Delver's Bestiary

*A field journal of what lives below — and how it eats, and how it is eaten.*

---

## Preface

I have kept this journal since my first descent, when I mistook a Mosslamb's bleat for a child's cry and nearly put a knife through my own foot backing away from it. Every page since has been paid for in bruises, singed eyebrows, and one memorable evening spent removing Spinefowl quills from my forearm by candlelight. What follows is not a bestiary in the museum sense — pinned wings under glass, Latin names no one down here will ever use. It is a hunter's ledger and a cook's notebook stitched into one, because in this dungeon the two trades are the same trade. Everything below eats something, and is eaten by something, and if you are patient and hungry enough, that something can be you standing over a fire wondering how best to season it. I have tried to record what each creature looks like at a glance — the shape you see before you decide whether to run — how it behaves when the deep is calm and how it changes when the deep is angry, what it will do to you if you are careless, and what it tastes like if you are not. Take this journal deeper than I have. Add to it. And for your own sake: never, ever go looking for the Stag.

# TIER 1 — GRAZERS & FORAGERS

*The prey base. They eat moss, fungus, roots, and each other's leftovers, and everything above them eats them. Handle gently — a Tier 1 collapse starves the whole floor above it.*

## Mosslamb

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer. **Floors:** 1 (Fungal Shallows), 2 (The Rootways).

**Diet:** Cave moss and lichen.

**Appearance:** Barrel-bodied low-poly quadruped, a stack of smooth rounded boulders under a felted mossy coat. Palette: chalky grey-green stone, matte green-brown fur, a faint teal glow where lichen has rooted on its back. Distinctive feature: blunt moss-tufted horn-stubs, permanently half-lidded eyes.

**Behaviour:** Placid, grazes in loose clusters, freezes stiff-legged before trotting off in a dumb straight line. Under rising hostility it startles at greater range and the herd bolts together, sometimes toward danger rather than away from it.

**Danger:** Essentially none. A cornered Mosslamb headbutts weakly; the risk is tripping over one, or waking whatever was stalking the herd.

**On the plate:** Thick, tender flanks, generous yield. Roasts beautifully with no special prep — the reliable Shallows meal.

**Ecology:** Base prey for Gloomferret, Ashjackal, Tunnel Constrictor. A Mosslamb crash lets moss choke the Shallows.

## Grotto Springhare

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Fungus caps and fallen fruit.

**Appearance:** Long-legged, big-eared low-poly hare, sharp angular haunches, a coiled-spring silhouette. Palette: pale limestone-grey fading to cream belly, ink-dark ear tips, bright amber eyes. No bioluminescence — it survives on speed and shadow, not glow.

**Behaviour:** Constant nibble-freeze-bound motion, extremely aware even calm. Under rising hostility it skips the freeze entirely — pure zig-zag flight into open ground, sometimes straight past the delver.

**Danger:** None in a fight; it will not fight. The danger is the chase, which burns your stamina while something with teeth notices the commotion.

**On the plate:** Lean, modest yield. Spit-roast fast over high heat before it toughens — a hard-won little meal.

**Ecology:** Prey for Ashjackal packs, Hookbeak Ridgehawk, and young Rackjaw. Speed is its only defense against three hunters.

## Capglow Snail

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer/Detritivore. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Moss and decaying matter.

**Appearance:** Slow, heavy low-poly form: a broad basalt-grey foot beneath a spiral shell whose ridges glow soft teal-violet from within — pretty in the dark, which is exactly why predators know where it is. Rounded, glowing, entirely defenseless in shape.

**Behaviour:** Barely reacts; withdraws and waits. It cannot flee, so even at high hostility its only gear is "hide harder" — the one creature whose danger never scales with the world's mood.

**Danger:** None directly. The real risk is the slime — handled carelessly it fouls a blade and any meat it touches.

**On the plate:** Rich, dense meat once de-slimed — this requires working Sulfur Chive through the flesh first, or the dish carries a mucus off-taste and a stomach penalty.

**Ecology:** Eaten by Gloomferret, Grave Otter, Rockback Boar. Its detritivory recycles dead matter across Floors 1–2.

## Blind Vole

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Roots, tubers, and low herbs.

**Appearance:** Small, featureless low-poly ovoid with stub limbs, no visible eyes, a blunt whiskered snout. Palette: pale grubby pink-grey with faint dirt-brown mottling — deliberately the least interesting silhouette in the dungeon, built to survive on being beneath notice.

**Behaviour:** Digs and burrows constantly, surfacing only to forage. Reacts to vibration, not sight. Under rising hostility it simply burrows deeper and surfaces less — hiding from a hostile world rather than fighting it.

**Danger:** None. Cannot meaningfully harm a delver.

**On the plate:** Small, clean yield — no toxins, no spines, no slime. Best simmered into stock; several Voles make a proper pot.

**Ecology:** Staple prey for Gloomferret and Cinder Cockatril. Its foraging lightly manages tuber density in the upper floors.

## Palefish

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer, aquatic. **Floors:** 1, 2, 3 (abundant in The Sunless Marsh).

**Diet:** Algae and larvae.

**Appearance:** Slim, translucent-pale low-poly fish shape, near-white with blue-violet undertones from internal bioluminescent organs. Ghostly, glass-like, schooling in drifting clusters of soft cold light. No aggressive features — a creature of stillness and shadow.

**Behaviour:** Schools tightly, scatters explosively in all directions when disturbed. Under rising hostility schools thin fast — every aquatic predator on the web depends on Palefish, so it crashes hardest under pressure.

**Danger:** None. Cannot be fought and does not attack.

**On the plate:** Delicate flesh, falls apart if overcooked — pan-sear briefly or steam. Pairs naturally with Marrow Mint, which cold-resists the eater and cuts the marsh-water edge.

**Ecology:** Foundational prey for Grave Otter, Bog Saurian, Deepwater Leviathan-eel. A Palefish crash starves the whole aquatic chain within a season.

## Deep Quail

**Tier & Role:** Tier 1 — Grazer. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Seeds and insects.

**Appearance:** Plump, ground-hugging low-poly bird, short stubby legs, a stiff fan tail, mottled slate-and-rust plumage in flat color bands — reads instantly as small and harmless. No glow; relies on leaf-litter camouflage.

**Behaviour:** Skittish, bursts into short low flights rather than sustained flight when startled. Under rising hostility it flushes at greater distance and more explosively, and abandoned nests grow more common as hens are pressured.

**Danger:** None from the bird; the hazard is the noise of a flushed covey drawing in something bigger.

**On the plate:** Small carcass, whole-roasted simply. Its eggs are the real prize — rich, risk-free, and excellent in almost any recipe needing binding or richness.

**Ecology:** Prey for Gloomferret, Ashjackal, Cinder Cockatril, and Hookbeak Ridgehawk from above.

# TIER 2 — SMALL HUNTERS & OMNIVORES

*The first real predators, plus one armored charger and one aquatic scavenger. All of them can hurt a careless delver in a small, personal way.*

## Gloomferret

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Capglow Snail, Blind Vole, Deep Quail.

**Appearance:** Long, low, sinuous low-poly body built around speed through tight spaces — elongated torso, short legs, whip-thin tail. Palette: charcoal-black fur with a single dull violet bioluminescent stripe spine-to-tail, just enough glow to track it as a moving line in the dark.

**Behaviour:** Bold for its size, investigates the delver's scent before deciding whether to bother. Under rising hostility it stops retreating from confrontation, darting in for opportunistic bites and sometimes trailing at a distance like a scavenger-in-waiting.

**Danger:** Fast and low, hard to land a clean hit on; chains quick ankle-nips before darting off. The real threat is numbers, or being harried while something bigger closes in.

**On the plate:** Gamey meat that resists simple roasting — braise slow with a root or herb to tame the flavor into something good.

**Ecology:** Predator of Capglow Snail, Blind Vole, Deep Quail; prey for Gloamstalker Lynx and Tunnel Constrictor.

## Ashjackal

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter, pack. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Grotto Springhare, Deep Quail.

**Appearance:** Lean, angular low-poly canid — sharp shoulder ridges, narrow snout, alert triangular ears built for pack coordination at a glance. Palette: ash-grey coat with sooty smudging along the flanks, small amber eye-glow rather than full bioluminescence.

**Behaviour:** Never hunts alone by choice; packs flank fleeing prey. A clear hostility gauge — calm-world packs are small and break off losing fights, while hostile-world packs grow bolder, larger, and press attacks a calm pack would abandon.

**Danger:** Individually weak but they flank — one circles while another holds you, and a full pack drains stamina through sheer attack volume. Watch for the pack thinning; that's the flank forming.

**On the plate:** Tough meat that wants long cooking — stewing is far more forgiving than roasting.

**Ecology:** Predator of Springhare and Deep Quail; prey for Gloamstalker Lynx, Marrow Hyena, Rackjaw. Pack boldness is a visible hostility thermometer.

## Rockback Boar

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter, omnivore. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Roots, Capglow Snail, carrion.

**Appearance:** Squat, heavy-shouldered low-poly boar with a broad back armored in overlapping stone-grey hexagonal plates — the "rockback." Palette: slate plating over warm brown-black hide, two small curved tusks catching firelight amber. No glow; built to look solid, not pretty.

**Behaviour:** Grubs at a slow, distracted pace, mostly ignoring the delver until provoked or carrion-scented. Under rising hostility it reads proximity itself as provocation, and its charge trigger-range extends.

**Danger:** The charge — a telegraphed stamp-and-snort into a fast linear rush that staggers and gores a flat-footed delver. Sidestep it; the Boar takes a moment to reorient, which is the punish window.

**On the plate:** Excellent fatty meat, one of the best straightforward roasts on Floor 1–2, rendering its own fat for other cuts.

**Ecology:** Predator/scavenger of roots and Capglow Snail; prey for Gloamstalker Lynx, Marrow Hyena, Bog Saurian, Gravemaw Ursine. Carrion-eating means uncleaned kills draw it in.

## Spinefowl

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Insects, Palefish, eggs.

**Appearance:** Tall, gawky low-poly ground bird, more upright than Deep Quail, with a ridge of stiff quill-spines down its back rendered as sharp angular triangles. Palette: dull rust-brown body, the spine-ridge in pale bone-white as its one visibly dangerous tell. No glow.

**Behaviour:** Forages actively near water, aggressive toward competing foragers. Under rising hostility its spine-bristling threat display gives way faster to the actual spine strike.

**Danger:** Launches a short-range spiky jab when threatened — modest damage, but fragments sting if you grapple or butcher it carelessly.

**On the plate:** Fine poultry once spines are carefully stripped in butchery — skip that and it's unpleasant, even risks a lodged-spine penalty.

**Ecology:** Predator of insects and Palefish; prey for Hookbeak Ridgehawk and Chasm Drake.

## Grave Otter

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter, aquatic. **Floors:** 2, 3 (The Sunless Marsh).

**Diet:** Palefish, Capglow Snail.

**Appearance:** Sleek, elongated low-poly otter form, fast and low-slung in water, a pale grey-white face patch against near-black wet-looking hide. Cold-violet bioluminescence rims its whiskers and eye-patch — an eerie glowing face just above the waterline in dark pools.

**Behaviour:** Purposeful, hunts in short dives, territorial along its stretch of water. Under rising hostility it defends territory directly against the delver rather than simply diving away, even in shallows.

**Danger:** Fast and slippery in water, where it has every advantage; slow and manageable on land. Repeated dives make it hard to pin down in its element.

**On the plate:** Oily, rich meat suited to a warming stew far better than dry roasting — the fat renders straight into the broth.

**Ecology:** Predator of Palefish and Capglow Snail; prey for Bog Saurian and Deepwater Leviathan-eel. Thin Otters signal a thinning Palefish population.

## Cinder Cockatril

**Tier & Role:** Tier 2 — Small Hunter. **Floors:** 1, 2.

**Diet:** Deep Quail, Blind Vole.

**Appearance:** Compact, upright low-poly bird-reptile hybrid, cockerel-postured, a low crest shifting from dull ash-grey to smoldering orange-red — banked coals in flat color blocks. The warm color is the toxicity tell, not decoration.

**Behaviour:** Struts and displays before engaging. Under rising hostility that ratio flips — it strikes far sooner and warns far less.

**Danger:** Bite and scratch carry a mild toxin — survivable but debilitating in a longer fight, especially against something bigger it drew in.

**On the plate:** Mildly toxic raw — work Sulfur Chive into the meat during prep and it cooks up safe and good, with a faint smoky edge. Skip the Chive and the dish is dangerous.

**Ecology:** Predator of Deep Quail and Blind Vole; prey for larger Tier 3 hunters. An early lesson in "prep before you eat."

# TIER 3 — MID PREDATORS

*Real threats now. Ambush, packs, aerial harassment, and reptiles with real bite force. This is where a careless delver starts dying.*

## Gloamstalker Lynx

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator. **Floors:** 2, 3.

**Diet:** Gloomferret, Ashjackal pups, Deep Quail.

**Appearance:** Lean, long-limbed low-poly big cat, low to the ground with an exaggerated stalking posture — flattened haunches, low head carriage, angular shoulder blades that read as tension even standing still. Palette: mottled dark stone-grey and near-black, faint cold teal flecks along the ruff that catch light only when it's already close.

**Behaviour:** A patient, silent stalker that closes distance in cover before committing; baseline it abandons a stalk if spotted early. Under rising hostility it commits from far closer range and rarely breaks off once noticed — the stalk becomes a near-certain pounce.

**Danger:** The pounce is the whole fight — a heavy leaping strike that staggers poise if you miss the tell (a brief crouch-and-tail-flick). Miss it once and it's manageable in the open.

**On the plate:** Lean cuts — sear hot and fast; slow-cooking dries it out.

**Ecology:** Predator of Gloomferret, Ashjackal pups, Deep Quail; prey for Chasm Drake. A real check on Ashjackal pack growth.

## Hookbeak Ridgehawk

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator, aerial. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4 (The Bonefields).

**Diet:** Grotto Springhare, Spinefowl.

**Appearance:** Broad-winged low-poly raptor with an exaggerated hooked beak and stiff angular wings built for cavern updrafts. Palette: dusty ridge-brown plumage, pale cream chest, a faint amber eye-glow that reads as a hunting stare from far off — the one Tier 3 predator you might spot before it spots you, if you look up.

**Behaviour:** Circles ridgelines and ceilings watching for movement, strikes in a fast diving pass rather than ground combat. Under rising hostility it hunts lower and bolder, making repeated passes instead of one strike-and-retreat.

**Danger:** Hard to reach airborne — the real danger is the dive itself, a fast talon strike with little warning beyond a shadow and rushing air, capable of knocking a delver down.

**On the plate:** A difficult kill to bring down cleanly, but good lean meat once you do — pan-sear to avoid drying it further.

**Ecology:** Predator of Springhare and Spinefowl; prey for Cavern Roc.

## Marrow Hyena

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator, pack. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4.

**Diet:** Rockback Boar, Ashjackal, carrion.

**Appearance:** Hunched, powerful-jawed low-poly canid, heavier through the skull than an Ashjackal, dropped-hindquarter stance. Palette: bone-white and marrow-yellow blotching over dull grey-brown coat — a color scheme that echoes what it's known for eating. No glow; its low rolling call carries far instead.

**Behaviour:** Scavenges opportunistically, hunts in coordinated packs when carrion is scarce, and is strongly drawn to unbutchered kills. Under rising hostility packs grow larger, drawn from further away, and press fights far past where a calm pack would quit.

**Danger:** Bone-cracking bite force among the hardest-hitting Tier 3 bites, plus pack tactics that isolate a delver. Leaving a kill nearby can turn one Hyena into four.

**On the plate:** Tough, sinewy meat that rewards long slow braising — a filling meal, not a quick one.

**Ecology:** Predator/scavenger of Rockback Boar, Ashjackal, carrion; commanded by Elder Marrowmother; prey for Gravemaw Ursine and Pale Sabertooth. The clearest lesson that waste has teeth.

## Bog Saurian

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator, semi-aquatic. **Floors:** 3.

**Diet:** Grave Otter, Palefish, Rockback Boar.

**Appearance:** Low, wide-bodied low-poly reptile, most of its bulk riding low in murky water with only ridged spine and eyes breaking the surface — a classic "is that a log" profile. Palette: mottled swamp-green and mud-brown scales, a sickly teal glow-stripe down the tail visible even underwater — the only warning before it moves.

**Behaviour:** Motionless for long stretches, explosively fast in the final lunge. Baseline it ambushes only at the water's edge. Under rising hostility it pursues further onto shore than it should be able to, shrinking the delver's safe margin.

**Danger:** The lunge-and-drag — a sudden burst from stillness into a jaw clamp that can pull a delver toward deep water, where the fight strongly favors the Saurian.

**On the plate:** The tail is the prize cut — dense, excellent meat, best grilled directly over flame.

**Ecology:** Predator of Grave Otter, Palefish, Rockback Boar; prey for Dire Basilisk and Deepwater Leviathan-eel.

## Tunnel Constrictor

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator, ambush. **Floors:** 2, 3.

**Diet:** Blind Vole, Gloomferret, Grotto Springhare.

**Appearance:** Enormously long, thick-bodied low-poly serpent, coils rendered as smooth overlapping cylindrical segments, reading clearly against rock when coiled in a crevice. Palette: stone-grey and root-brown patterning that mimics tunnel wall texture almost exactly, with two small cold-violet glow-points for eyes — the one part that never quite blends in.

**Behaviour:** Coils motionless in wall crevices for extended stretches. Baseline it strikes only at close range and only things its own size or smaller. Under rising hostility it strikes at greater range and at targets — including the delver — well above what it would normally risk.

**Danger:** The strike-and-wrap — a fast lunge into a constricting grapple that costs real stamina to break. The tell is subtle: a slight shift in what looked like a root or crack.

**On the plate:** Huge meat yield relative to effort — good grilled in thick coiled sections.

**Ecology:** Predator of Blind Vole, Gloomferret, Springhare; prey for Dire Basilisk.

## Rackjaw

**Tier & Role:** Tier 3 — Mid Predator, pack. **Floors:** 2, 3.

**Diet:** Grotto Springhare, Deep Quail, Gloamstalker Lynx cubs.

**Appearance:** Tall, rangy low-poly wolf-elk hybrid — wolf-like body topped with a branching antler rack in sharp angular geometry, an unmistakable predator profile. Palette: cold slate-grey coat, bone-white antlers that catch any light and make a pack visible before detail resolves.

**Behaviour:** Runs fast, coordinated packs, running prey down rather than ambushing. Under rising hostility packs run larger and pursue a fleeing delver much further than a calm pack would bother.

**Danger:** Speed is the core threat — outrunning a pack isn't realistic, and the antlers add goring to a bite-and-tackle attack. Fight near cover to negate their advantage.

**On the plate:** Lean, flavorful meat, roasts well over a hot fire; the antlers are a useful craft material.

**Ecology:** Predator of Springhare, Deep Quail, young Lynx; prey for Pale Sabertooth and Cavern Roc.

# TIER 4 — LARGE PREDATORS

*Feast-or-nightmare territory. Everything here can end a run. Everything here also feeds you for days if you win.*

## Chasm Drake

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Large Predator. **Floors:** 3, 4.

**Diet:** Hookbeak Ridgehawk, Gloamstalker Lynx, Marrow Hyena.

**Appearance:** A wingless wyvern silhouette, long and hind-heavy with vestigial folded wing-limbs used as slashing forelimbs — a genuinely alien low-poly profile among the roster's more mammalian creatures. Palette: dark volcanic-black scale plating, veins of banked-ember orange glowing faintly along ribs and jaw — heat you see before you feel it.

**Behaviour:** Territorial, holds ground, vents small heat bursts as a warning before committing. Under rising hostility the warning shortens dramatically and it closes distance far more readily.

**Danger:** Bite and claw carry residual heat damage — fight too close too long and your gear and health both cook slowly between hits. Wingless, so it can't follow into tight vertical terrain.

**On the plate:** A genuine feast, but the meat needs proper searing, not light cooking — its hot biology carries through raw; undercooked it's acrid.

**Ecology:** Predator of Ridgehawk, Lynx, Marrow Hyena; prey for The Gloom Tyrant and Cavern Roc.

## Gravemaw Ursine

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Large Predator. **Floors:** 3, 4.

**Diet:** Marrow Hyena, Rockback Boar, Bog Saurian.

**Appearance:** A massive, hunch-shouldered low-poly cave-bear silhouette built from broad blocky masses — the largest ground silhouette below Tier 5. Palette: deep charcoal-black fur shot through with pale bone-grey scarring, small deep-set amber eyes rather than any glow.

**Behaviour:** Slow to anger, content foraging carrion and hunting Hyena packs unless threatened. Under rising hostility its provocation threshold drops sharply — mere proximity starts to read as threat.

**Danger:** Overwhelming damage per hit and a stagger-heavy moveset that punishes standing ground — patient spacing and full commitment to the dodge is the only way through. A feast if you win, a nightmare if you don't respect it.

**On the plate:** Enormous, rich, fatty yield — the single most filling roast on Floors 3–4.

**Ecology:** Predator of Marrow Hyena, Rockback Boar, Bog Saurian; prey only for The Gloom Tyrant.

## Pale Sabertooth

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Large Predator. **Floors:** 4.

**Diet:** Rackjaw, Gravemaw Ursine cubs, Chasm Drake young.

**Appearance:** Lean, long-fanged low-poly big cat built for speed over mass — narrow-waisted, with two dramatically oversized curved fangs as its signature feature, rendered as clean sharp wedges. Palette: near-white to pale bone-grey coat with cold violet bioluminescent striping — a striking, ghostly profile against the dark Bonefields.

**Behaviour:** A glass-cannon hunter, fast and constantly repositioning rather than tanking hits. Under rising hostility it presses attacks after being wounded, where a calm Sabertooth would disengage.

**Danger:** Extremely fast, hard-hitting strikes with poor defense of its own — a clean counter does real damage, but its opening lunge is often fatal on its own if it lands.

**On the plate:** The hide is genuinely prized craft material; the meat, lean, is excellent quickly seared.

**Ecology:** Predator of Rackjaw and of Ursine/Drake young; prey for The Gloom Tyrant and Cavern Roc.

## Dire Basilisk

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Large Predator, toxin. **Floors:** 3, 4.

**Diet:** Bog Saurian, Rackjaw, Tunnel Constrictor.

**Appearance:** Heavy, low-slung low-poly reptile with a distinctive frilled crest along neck and skull, flat sickly yellow-green against a dull stone-grey scaled body — an immediate "do not touch" read. A venomous teal-green glow pulses at the throat before it strikes — a genuine, learnable tell.

**Behaviour:** Slow and deliberate, relying on toxin over speed, reluctant to chase far. Under rising hostility it pursues and strikes preemptively far more readily.

**Danger:** Bite and spit both carry a serious toxin — stacked exposure without treatment incapacitates before physical damage alone would. The throat-glow is your entire margin of safety.

**On the plate:** Toxin glands must be removed or neutralized with Sulfur Chive during prep. Properly handled, a genuine trophy dish.

**Ecology:** Predator of Bog Saurian, Rackjaw, Tunnel Constrictor.

## Deepwater Leviathan-eel

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Large Predator, aquatic. **Floors:** 3.

**Diet:** Bog Saurian, Grave Otter, aquatic species broadly.

**Appearance:** Enormous, ribbon-long low-poly eel, its body coiled beneath dark water with only a length of scaled back breaking the surface — deliberately hard to fully read until it's already attacking. Palette: deep near-black hide, a row of cold teal-violet bioluminescent spots glowing beneath the water even when the body isn't visible.

**Behaviour:** Patrols deep water territorially, hidden, striking rather than pursuing on land. Under rising hostility it patrols closer to shorelines, shrinking the delver's safe margin.

**Danger:** A genuine terror of the Marsh — its strike from calm water gives almost no warning, and a grip in deep water strongly favors it.

**On the plate:** Enormous, oily yield, among the largest single-creature harvests below Tier 5 — good slow-rendered for its fat.

**Ecology:** Apex-adjacent predator of Bog Saurian, Grave Otter, and aquatic prey broadly; unchallenged in deep water short of Tier 5.

## Stonehide Gorehorn

**Tier & Role:** Tier 4 — Mega-herbivore. **Floors:** 3, 4.

**Diet:** Ironbark Deeproot shoots and heavy flora.

**Appearance:** Colossal plated low-poly rhino, flat armored slabs and one massive horn-wedge — the largest herbivore here, built to look immovable. Palette: weathered stone-grey, lichen-green staining at the seams, no bioluminescence — it needn't glow to be respected.

**Behaviour:** Placid undisturbed, gives extended warning before charging. Under rising hostility that window shortens — still no predator, but quicker to read presence as provocation.

**Danger:** Not a predator, but the charge is lethal, effectively untouchable — respect distance, never tank or outrun it.

**On the plate:** Killing an adult isn't realistic; avoidance is the intent, not a trophy hunt.

**Ecology:** Consumer of Ironbark Deeproot; prey only for Titan Molebeast.

# TIER 5 — APEX

*The bottom of the food chain has nothing above these. Most delvers will never fight one on purpose — and one of them should never be fought at all.*

## The Gloom Tyrant

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Apex Predator. **Floors:** 4, 5.

**Diet:** Chasm Drake, Gravemaw Ursine, Pale Sabertooth.

**Appearance:** Towering, heavily built low-poly apex silhouette that dwarfs even Gravemaw Ursine — broad-chested, thick-limbed, the natural endpoint of everything below it in scale. Palette: near-total black hide, a crown of cold violet bioluminescent markings around head and shoulders, unmistakable even at long range in total darkness.

**Behaviour:** Holds vast territory, largely ignores anything smaller than Tier 4. Under rising hostility — especially a Bonefields/Maw spike — it investigates disturbances it would otherwise dismiss.

**Danger:** Everything about it is dangerous: reach, damage, a moveset that punishes any delver engaging without peak preparation. A fight to route around, not seek.

**On the plate:** A legendary feast for the rare delver equipped to take one — not a realistic goal for most descents.

**Ecology:** Apex predator of Chasm Drake, Gravemaw Ursine, Pale Sabertooth. Caps Tier 4 growth from above; no natural predator of its own.

## Elder Marrowmother

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Apex, matriarch. **Floors:** 4.

**Diet:** Rackjaw, Stonehide Gorehorn calves, rival hyenas.

**Appearance:** An outsized low-poly Marrow Hyena, scarred and heavy-shouldered where lesser Hyenas are lean, a mane of pale bone-white fur along the neck marking her rank at a glance. Palette: the same bone-and-marrow blotching, darker and more saturated, a commanding amber eye-glow.

**Behaviour:** Rarely fights directly — her danger is what she commands. Under rising hostility her packs grow larger and press engagements far past an ordinary pack's breaking point.

**Danger:** Almost never her alone — the coordinated pack she leads executes flanks and sustained pressure an ordinary pack can't manage.

**On the plate:** Rarely hunted, rarely worth it — surviving her pack is the point, not the meal.

**Ecology:** Matriarch and commander of Marrow Hyena packs; predator of Rackjaw and Gorehorn calves. Her loss would likely fragment regional pack structure — a small echo of the Hollow Stag's role.

## Cavern Roc

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Apex, aerial. **Floors:** 4, 5.

**Diet:** Chasm Drake, Pale Sabertooth, Rackjaw.

**Appearance:** A genuinely massive low-poly raptor, wings spanning wider than most chambers feel comfortable with — the Hookbeak Ridgehawk's design language scaled into something that blots out firelight passing overhead. Palette: deep ridge-brown and bone-cream plumage, a warm amber eye-glow visible before the shape is.

**Behaviour:** Hunts vast aerial territory, taking large ground prey in devastating dives. Under rising hostility it ranges lower and hunts closer to disturbed ground.

**Danger:** The dive is close to unsurvivable if it connects on an unprepared delver. The only real counter is cover — overhangs, tight chambers, anything denying it the open dive.

**On the plate:** A trophy hunt in every sense — bringing one down is the achievement; the meat is almost beside the point.

**Ecology:** Apex aerial predator of Chasm Drake, Pale Sabertooth, Rackjaw. A real check on large predators venturing into open ground.

## The Sunless Wyrm

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Apex, Floor 5 dread. **Floors:** 5 (The Maw), exclusively.

**Diet:** Everything. Not a specialist — the reason Floor 5 has an apex at all.

**Appearance:** Immense serpent-dragon in flat overlapping low-poly plates along an impossibly long coiled body, never fully visible at once. Palette: a hide dark enough to read as negative space against the Maw's minimal light, threaded with veins of cold violet-white glow that pulse like breathing.

**Behaviour:** The Maw's dread made literal — its presence shapes every other Floor 5 creature's behaviour. Rising Floor 5 hostility is widely believed to shorten how long it stays dormant, though few who'd confirm it have returned.

**Danger:** No readable tells here, because surviving it isn't, by design, about combat skill. If you see it, you have already erred. Distance, darkness, and prayer, in that order.

**On the plate:** No delver has returned from butchering one. This journal has no entry to give, and hopes it never needs one.

**Ecology:** The Maw's apex predator, preying on everything present without exception. No predator, functionally no equal.

## Titan Molebeast

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Apex, burrower. **Floors:** 4, 5.

**Diet:** Stonehide Gorehorn, Gravemaw Ursine.

**Appearance:** Colossal, thick, rounded low-poly burrowing silhouette, almost architectural in its blockiness, built around massive shovel-like foreclaws that dwarf its already huge frame. Palette: pale subterranean grey-white — the color of something that has never surfaced — with dull amber glow-flecks along the claws from packed mineral dust.

**Behaviour:** Spends most of its time underground, surfacing rarely and explosively. Under rising hostility on deeper floors it's believed to surface more often near disturbed ground, though direct observation is rare.

**Danger:** Being caught on ground about to breach is close to a death sentence on its own, before it even attacks. Once surfaced, its size and claws make it a genuine open-ground threat.

**On the plate:** Not a realistic hunt for most delvers — this entry exists for completeness and awe.

**Ecology:** Burrower-predator of Stonehide Gorehorn and Gravemaw Ursine — one of the few things that can threaten an adult Gorehorn. Its burrowing reshapes terrain.

## The Hollow Stag

**Tier & Role:** Tier 5 — Keystone, NOT a predator. **Floors:** 4, 5 — sighted rarely on both.

**Diet:** High flora only — the oldest, tallest growth in the deepest reaches.

**Appearance:** A tall, still low-poly stag, antlers in geometry too clean to be anything but deliberate. Palette: bone-white hide, a constant violet-white glow along antlers and spine, the calmest light down here. It does not look dangerous. That is the point.

**Behaviour:** Grazes alone, reacting to a delver the way nothing else does — no flee, no charge, no display. It seems to be the one constant the ecosystem reacts around, not to.

**Danger:** The Stag will not hurt you. Everything after you hurt it will — a consequence, not an attack, and the worst one down here.

**On the plate:** Do not. No recipe, no yield — no version where eating it is worth what follows.

**Ecology:** Not meaningfully prey to anything recorded. Its presence stabilises the food web; killing it triggers a severe hostility spike and a long recovery. Leave it be.

# THE TEN INSECTS

*Not part of the trophic tiers — pervasive hazards that even Tier 1–2 animals hunt for food. They will not feed you much, but they will absolutely kill you if you treat them as background noise.*

## Razorwing Wasp

**Threat:** A fast, humming flying swarm, wings catching a thin red-amber shimmer; its stings open shallow, persistent bleeding wounds rather than one big hit, and several stings together add up fast. **Floors:** 1, 2, 3. **Avoid/Counter:** Smoke and fire disperse a swarm quickly; retreat through a narrow gap rather than fighting it in open ground. **Use:** Negligible culinary value; best routed around, not harvested.

## Glowmite Swarm

**Threat:** A drifting cluster of tiny biters, each carrying its own pinprick of cold light; together they dazzle and disorient on contact — the real danger is whatever finds you while you're blinded. **Floors:** 1, 2, 3. **Avoid/Counter:** Look away the instant you spot the glow-cluster; never engage it while another threat is nearby. **Use:** None known; a pure visibility hazard.

## Bloodtick Crawler

**Threat:** A small, dark, unremarkable crawler that latches on unnoticed and drains health steadily over time rather than striking outright — the danger creeps up on you long after you've stopped thinking about it. **Floors:** 1, 2, 3. **Avoid/Counter:** Check yourself after dense undergrowth or low crawlspaces; pull an attached tick free early, before the drain compounds. **Use:** None; a nuisance best prevented, not fought.

## Cinder Beetle

**Threat:** A slow, glowing-bellied beetle that looks harmless right up until the killing blow — it detonates on death into a genuine burn radius, punishing anyone who finished it up close. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4. **Avoid/Counter:** Kill it from range where possible, or step back immediately after any melee finish. **Use:** Its detonation, triggered deliberately from safe range, can clear a path through denser insect hazards.

## Chitin Scuttler

**Threat:** Heavily armored beneath a hard, overlapping plated shell and genuinely slow-moving — low individual danger, but tanky enough that fighting it head-on wastes far more stamina than it's worth. **Floors:** 1, 2, 3, 4. **Avoid/Counter:** Simply outpace it; its slow speed makes avoidance nearly free in almost any terrain. **Use:** Minimal; not a meaningful food source relative to the toughness of the shell.

## Venomfang Centipede

**Threat:** Long, fast, and genuinely dangerous for its size — its poison bite stacks quickly if it lands more than once, and it closes distance faster than most delvers expect from something this small. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4. **Avoid/Counter:** Running isn't reliable at its speed; fight it early and clean rather than letting it chase, and treat poison stacks immediately. **Use:** None safely known; the venom glands are best avoided entirely.

## Gravel Mantis

**Threat:** Camouflages almost perfectly against loose stone and scattered rubble, holding dead still, then bursts out with a high-damage ambush strike that gives almost no warning at all. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4. **Avoid/Counter:** Move deliberately through rubble fields rather than sprinting through them blind; a slight color mismatch in the stone is the only tell before it commits. **Use:** None; purely a read-and-avoid ambush hazard.

## Deathcap Gnat Cloud

**Threat:** A drifting cloud of gnats around a haze of pale spores, inflicting a slow poison-over-time on anyone who lingers inside rather than delivering any single direct attack. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4, especially near dense fungal growth. **Avoid/Counter:** Simply don't walk into or linger in the visible haze; if caught inside, move out along the shortest path rather than trying to fight through it. **Use:** None edible; the spores themselves are the entire hazard.

## Spinneret Lurker

**Threat:** A large tunnel-dwelling spider that strings webbing across mouths and ceilings and immobilises the delver on contact — the true danger is rarely the bite itself, but whatever finds you while you're stuck. **Floors:** 2, 3, 4. **Avoid/Counter:** Watch for strung webbing before entering a tunnel; if caught, prioritize breaking free immediately over any other action. **Use:** No culinary value, but its silk has plausible utility beyond the kitchen.

## Corpsefly Cloud

**Threat:** Swarms carrion, spreading rot and raising local attention — it can draw in Marrow Hyenas and other scavengers. **Floors:** 1 through 5, wherever a carcass sits uncleaned. **Avoid/Counter:** Butcher and clear kills promptly; a Corpsefly Cloud is a symptom of waste, not a random hazard. **Use:** None — its presence itself is a warning.
